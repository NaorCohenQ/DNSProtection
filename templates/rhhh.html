The Randomized Hierarchical Heavy Hitters (RHHH) algorithm is designed to efficiently detect heavy hitters within hierarchical structures, such as IP address ranges in network traffic. It works by identifying prefixes (e.g., /8, /16, /24) that generate a significant amount of traffic and might indicate potential sources of DDoS attacks or other anomalies.

RHHH operates by sampling and tracking the most significant traffic sources across multiple hierarchical levels, allowing for a scalable and efficient method to detect network threats in real-time. This is particularly useful in environments where large volumes of network traffic must be monitored continuously.

Key Features:

  • Identifies heavy hitters across hierarchical levels.
  • Efficiently processes large volumes of data.
  • Scalable to handle diverse network traffic patterns.
  • Can be used to detect potential DDoS attacks.
